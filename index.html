I'm a 20 year old economics student at York University in Toronto. Prior to

my studies at York, I completed my elementary and secondary education in

French, graduating in 2023 from Jeunes Sans Frontières Secondary

School with a French Bilingual Diploma. This bilingual background has

shaped both my academic identity and my ability to engage with diverse

perspectives in global economic discourse. Throughout my studies at York

university I became deeply interested in the relation between global

economic policies and development of countries, specifically emerging

countries and globalization. The effect of free trade, protectionism, foreign

investments which global powers lead to establish a form of

neo-colonialism. This extends into the involvement of international

organizations such as IMF, World Bank, and WTO in the financial

management of emerging countries. My goal is to develop policies to

support emerging countries in developing long term economic resilience

during global integration.

Research

I'm interested in researching the effects of globalisation. How has globalization affected

income inequality and the effect of globalization on developing countries, if the impact is

positive or does it hurt the development of said country. Does globalization lead to

neo-colonialism?
